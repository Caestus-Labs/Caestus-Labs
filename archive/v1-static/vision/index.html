Where we're going

We're not building a peripheral. We're building the haptic layer — the missing third channel between sight, sound, and the world your body is actually in.

Today: forty-eight clutch points across the upper body. Tomorrow: every centimeter of skin, every joint, every grip — addressable, fast, light enough to forget.

The endpoint isn't a better game. It's a body that can move freely between worlds, and a virtual world that finally treats the body like it's there.

Presence

The world should push back. Force, friction, weight, recoil — read by the same nerves that read reality. Sub-4ms or it's not real.

Embodiment

The hardware should disappear. 980g today, 400g by 2028, a layer under your shirt by 2032. The body wears it; the body forgets it.

Openness

One protocol, every engine, every headset. Every developer can write force. The web of haptics, not another walled garden.

— Caestus Labs · founding doc · 2024
