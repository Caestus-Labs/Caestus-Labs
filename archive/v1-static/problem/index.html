002 / The Problem

Touch is
completely missing.

VR got sharp. Tracking got faster. Headsets got lighter. The hand reaches in — nothing reaches back.

Problem Statement

Modern XR hardware has solved sight and sound at near-photoreal fidelity.

But the body's largest sensory system — touch — is still entirely absent.

Users reach into virtual worlds and feel nothing. No weight, no resistance, no texture, no contact.

01 / Sight + Sound

Solved.

4K per eye, spatial audio, sub-20ms latency. The visual and auditory channels are effectively complete.

02 / Hands

Tracked, not felt.

Inside-out tracking renders the hand in-world, but every reach passes through solid objects. No force ever fires back.

03 / The Body

Left behind.

Skin, muscle, and proprioception receive zero input. Immersion ends at the wrist.
